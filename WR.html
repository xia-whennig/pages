XIA implementations of White Rabbit and PTP

Traditionally, time synchronization between multiple modules of digital data acquisition is accomplished by sharing clocks, clock reset signals, and triggers with dedicated cabling. However, technologies like the IEEE 1588 Precision Time Protocol (PTP) and White Rabbit (PTP high accuray profile) are sufficiently precise that their use is becoming feasible in nuclear physics applications.

XIA explored and implemented White Rabbit and/or PTP in the following boards and projects:

  • Pixie-Net XL: Detector readout electronics with White Rabbit
  • PZ-TIO (on OHWR) : Synchronized trigger I/O with White Rabbit
  • WRclkDB (on OHWR) : White Rabbit oscillators implemented on a small PCB
  • MZTIO : Synchronized trigger I/O with PTP
  • Pixie-Net PTP version (limited availability)
  • Network Time Synchronization for Radiation Detectors: Assorted publications
